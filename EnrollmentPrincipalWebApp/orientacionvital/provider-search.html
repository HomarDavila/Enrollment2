Provider Search

This section of the portal gives you the opportunity to do a search for providers for your records. In the end, you can save your selections and print them to have with you when you need them. Your selections will disappear when you leave this page. If you want to make your unique Insurer change today, start your provider search by clicking on the "Change of Insurer" box.

Selection of my Primary Physician
You need to select a primary care physician (PCP) before you can change Insurers. Use the Provider Search to find your primary care physician, specialist, hospital or other health care facility near you. Before selecting a new Insurer, make sure your primary care physician (PCP), hospital or other health care service providers are in the provider's network of the Insurer of your choice.

How to look for a provider.
(Spanish video only)

A primary care physician (PCP) is a doctor who is selected by you and who will assist you in your health care and refer you to a specialist when necessary.

When you enroll with an Insurer, you will need a primary care physician (PCP) to coordinate your medical care. Your PCP plays a very important role in your medical care and the health services you will receive. For this reason, you must choose a PCP by selecting any of the participating Insurers of the VITAL Health Plan.

It is important that you choose the PCP that is best for you. Remember that you need to select a primary physician (PCP) before you can make an insurance change. Here are some considerations to help you select a PCP:

Choose your primary doctor from the following options:

Adult beneficiaries Choose among the following types of practitioners as your PCP: general practice, family practice, internal medicine, or geriatric medicine.
Beneficiaries under age 21 Consider choosing a pediatrician as your PCP.
Females older than 12 years old Female Enrollees age twelve (12) and older have an option to directly choose an obstetrician/gynecologist (OB/GYN) as a PCP with their selected MCO.

To change your Insurer, click on the "Log in now" link.

If you want to use a new primary care physician (PCP) who is already in your assigned Insurer, you do not need to change Insurers.

If you want to go to a PCP or specialist who is not part of your Insurer, you will need to change Insurers. Remember, you can only make one change during the annual open enrollment period, so chose carefully. When changing Insurers, you will need to select a PCP. Also remember everyone in your family doesn't have to use the same Insurer.

To change your Insurer, click on the "Log in now" link.

You must find a PCP and a Specialist who are in the same Insurer. Remember that all providers must be contracted by your Insurer, that is, belong to your provider network.

Assistance and Rights of Enrollees

This portal provides general information related to the VITAL Health Plan. It does not provide details about eligibility requirements under the Medicaid Program, the VITAL Health Plan, benefits, exceptions, general terms or limitations of the health plan of the Government of Puerto Rico. Eligibility determinations for the VITAL Health Plan are made by the Puerto Rico Medicaid Program. This portal does not represent a contract between you and the Health Insurance Administration of Puerto Rico (ASES), the Puerto Rico Medicaid Program, or any of the participating Insurers (MCOs).

The information about the provider networks presented in this portal is provided to ASES by Insurers hired for the VITAL Health Plan. This website does not modify the rules, benefits, exceptions, general terms or limitations of the Medicaid Program or its Health Plan of the Government of Puerto Rico. Periodic updates of the information provided will be made in a period of 48 hours. You can request more recent information directly with Insurers.

For interpreting services in other languages, contact the VITAL Counseling Center at 1-833-253-7721 free of charge Monday through Friday from 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.

TTY/TDD service is available only for Audio Impaired at 1-866-280-2050 for Spanish and 1-866-280-2053 for English.
